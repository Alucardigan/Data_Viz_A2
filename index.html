The Australian Real Estate Bubble

Introduction

Australia has quickly become one of the most speculative real estate markets leading to prices to outpace income

Over the years, more of the states have become unaffordable. In 2002, the lowest median house price $156950 AUD in Tasmania.

In 2020, the median house price in Tasmania has jumped to $351375 AUD.

Signs of a bubble

Economists often use different metrics to identify when a market has a bubble

The most common metric would be to compare the Housing Price Index (HPI) to Consumer Price Index (CPI) or Work Price Index

HPI represents the housing affordability across Australia, CPI represents inflation while WPI represents the labour costs

How did we get here?

One of the main reasons for the current housing bubble is the lack of construction of new housing.
Property has become a speculative asset that contributes to the Australian GDP which incentivises compounding of housing prices. Changes to the Foreign Investment Review Board and COVID-19 further contributed to slower housing building
